INTERVIEW WITH

TIN] HOWARD

Get ready for Gotham’s most stylish crime story yet! Writer Tini Howard takes us inside Sirens: Love Hurts, a four-issue Black Label series that unites her
with artist Babs Tarr for an all-new tale of friendship, murder, and romance. Blending the moody, seasonal structure of Batman: The Long Halloween with the
emotional punch of Sex and the City, Sirens: Love Hurts follows Poison Ivy, Harley Quinn, Catwoman, and Black Canary through one unforgettable year in Gotham.

What was the origin story of this bool?

It actually started as a conversation with DC Editorial,
who had the idea for a Sirens story inspired by Sex
and the City. They said, “This is the book we want to do,
and we think these people should make it.” Of course,
I couldn’t say yes fast enough. | had just come off
Catwoman and Harley Quinn, and it felt like the perfect
way to keep exploring Gotham in our own little corner.

What can you tell fans about the new villain
debuting in the bool?

We're introducing a new Gotham villain named
Horoscope. She’s similar to Calendar Man, but with
a celestial motif. She’s sexy, dangerous, and a little
unhinged, the anti-girl’s girl. It’s been so fun to
craft a villain whose beliefs directly clash with our
Sirens’ values.

Who do you hope reads Sirens: Love Hurts?

Anyone who's ever loved these characters, or
anyone who just loves beautiful women being chaotic
together! It’s for people who grew up with the
animated series and want to see them in a story that’s
stylish, mature, and full of heart.

What makes Sirens: Love Hurts unique to Black Label?
The fun of a Black Label book is that it can exist a little
outside continuity. We see these women at an earlier
point in their lives—Harley has just broken up with
the Joker, Bat and Cat aren’t officially together, and
Dinah’s just gotten engaged to Ollie. It lets me play
with their emotions before everything’s fully formed.

Also the freedom that comes with being a Black Label
book is that we can be sexier. It’s still a DC comic,
there’s action and a serial killer to stop, but it’s
driven by relationships first. We wanted a story
where the romance and the friendship matter as
much as the action.

The book follows the Sirens through four seasons.
How did that structure come about?

I knew | wanted this to feel like a year in their lives. Four
girls, four seasons. Every issue is oversized and themed
around a different season, from summer wedding prep
to winter heartbreak. This calls back to stories like
The Long Halloween or Year One, and | wanted to bring
that same rhythm to a story about our Sirens and how
their friendship evolves.
