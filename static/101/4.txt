THIS PLACE BEGAN WITH HER.
MY FATHER FOUGHT FOR THE UNION. MY FIRST MEMORIES ARE OF MY MOTHER STANDING WATCH AT OUR FRONT WINDOW AWAITING HIS RETURN. LEE HAD SURRENDERED AT APPOMATTOX COURT HOUSE, YOU SEE, AND MY FATHER WAS LONG PAST DUE.
THE WAITING HAD TAKEN ITS COLL ON MY MOTHER.
IT HAD TAKEN IT TOLL ON ME.
BUT THIS NIGHT WOULD BE DIFFERENT. THIS NIGHT THE DARK SILHOUETTE OF A UNION SOLDIER MARCHED UP OUR WALK.
IT WAS HIM, MY MOTHER EXCLAIMED! AFTER ALL THIS TIME!
FATHER WAS HOME!
